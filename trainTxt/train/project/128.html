introduction the computer vision research laboratory was established in the computer science department at the university of massachusetts in 1974 with the goal of investigating the scientific principles underlying the construction of integrated vision systems and the application of vision to problems of realworld importance the emphasis of our work is on vision systems that are capable of functioning flexibly and robustly in complex changing environments research in the laboratory has been supported by federal agencies including nsf arpa afosr and onr as well as private industry the major focus of the laboratory has been on knowledgedriven image interpretation the recovery of 3d structure and motion from images and image sequences and on computer architectures for vision the research is performed in the context of taskoriented funded projects including arpa radius ugv learning rsta and parallel architectures efforts a unifying and unique theme within the laboratory has been integration encompassing the basic research methodologies as well as driving the development of vision system research environments research projects work in progress aerial image understanding
