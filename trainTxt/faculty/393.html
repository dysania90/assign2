Our most recent success on data supplied by the Child Welfare Department of the State of Washington is memoralized in the &lt;!WA46&gt;&lt;a href=""&gt;letter viewable by clicking here.&lt;/a&gt; &lt;p&gt; &lt;dt&gt;&lt;!WA47&gt;&lt;IMG ALT="x" SRC=""&gt;&lt;b&gt;META-LEARNING for Scalable Data Mining&lt;/b&gt; &lt;dd&gt;&lt;!WA48&gt;&lt;a href=""&gt;Meta-learning&lt;/a&gt; is a term we coined for an approach gaining in popularity in the DM/KDD community these days. The essential idea is to combine a number of separately learned classifiers or models in such a fashion that machine learning can be scaled to large (and inherently distributed) databases, and (ideally) accuracy can be boosted. But how does one combine separately learned classifiers? We consider that question as a learning problem and apply machine learning algorithms to "meta-learn" how underlying classifiers behave or correlate with eachother.
