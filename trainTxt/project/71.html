The TRAnsparent VirtuaL EnviRoment (Travler) project is one of several ARPA-funded research efforts within the UCLA Computer Science Department. This nomadic computing research effort focuses on developing models, prototyping systems software, and running experiments in support of those capabilities needed by a mobile user. In support of these capabilities, we are involved in the following activities: virtual networking, nomadic routing, wireless and nomadic systems simulation, nomadic systems modeling, predictive caching, user-level replication services, security encapsulation, and data consistency. Each activity is shown as a piece of the adjacent pie chart, and a full description of each activity is obtained by clicking on each piece. Some of these services are currently being built, some are in the design phase. Eventually, Travler will integrate those services that already exist in reasonable forms (wireless communication protocols, power management software, secure transmission of data and authentication services) with components produced by the project and built into the Travler framework. The Principal Investigators on the Travler mobile computing research team are: Dr. Leonard Kleinrock Dr. Gerald Popek Dr. Rajive Bagrodia Dr. Peter Reiher Team members have published several important papers that directly relate to research currently underway on Travler. The Travler research team is also conducting simultaneous ARPA-funded research on the following related topics: Large-Scale Filing and Data Management Environments (Ficus), Secure, Bilateral Replicated Distributed Filing (Truffles), Wireless Adaptive Mobile Information Systems (WAMIS) and Co-Base.
