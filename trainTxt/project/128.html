Date: Wed, 20 Nov 1996 19:51:07 GMT Server: NCSA/1.5.1 Last-modified: Mon, 18 Nov 1996 23:38:19 GMT Content-type: text/html Content-length: 6930 UMass Computer Vision - Home page Best viewed using Netscape 2.0 or 3.0 Introduction The Computer Vision Research Laboratory was established in the Computer Science Department at the University of Massachusetts in 1974 with the goal of investigating the scientific principles underlying the construction of integrated vision systems and the application of vision to problems of real-world importance. The emphasis of our work is on vision systems that are capable of functioning flexibly and robustly in complex changing environments. Research in the laboratory has been supported by federal agencies, including NSF, ARPA, AFOSR, and ONR, as well as private industry. The major focus of the laboratory has been on knowledge-driven image interpretation, the recovery of 3D structure and motion from images and image sequences, and on computer architectures for vision. The research is performed in the context of task-oriented funded projects, including ARPA, RADIUS, UGV, Learning, RSTA, and Parallel Architectures efforts. A unifying (and unique) theme within the laboratory has been integration, encompassing the basic research methodologies as well as driving the development of vision system research environments.
